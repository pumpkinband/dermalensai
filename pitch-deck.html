The Problem

Skin care in India runs into the same wall, everywhere.

Dermatologists are concentrated in a small number of cities. For most people, a skin concern means a long wait, a costly consultation, or — far more often — no consultation at all.

Geography

Specialist dermatological care is concentrated in major metros, leaving smaller towns and rural areas with little direct access.

Cost & time

An in-person specialist visit means booking, travel, and waiting — a high bar to clear for what might be a minor, easily-treated condition.

The fallback is worse

Faced with that barrier, people default to internet search or self-diagnosis — with no clinical grounding and no path to a real doctor.

The Solution

A screening you can do in a minute — with a real doctor behind it.

DermaLens AI turns a phone camera into a first opinion, and turns that first opinion into a conversation with a licensed doctor when it matters.

The Technology

Built to know when it doesn't know.

Most consumer skin-check tools are a single model returning a confident-sounding guess. DermaLens AI is a three-stage pipeline designed around the opposite instinct: validate the input, weigh two independent opinions, and hand off to a human when certainty runs out.

Stage 1

Input validation

A CLIP-based vision model checks the photo is genuinely a skin image before any diagnosis runs — filtering out irrelevant or unusable uploads at the door.

openai/clip-vit-base-patch32
→

Stage 2

Ensemble diagnosis

Two independently trained classifiers score the image across ~35 dermatological conditions. Low-confidence results are returned as "uncertain," not forced into a guess.

Stage 3

Human escalation

Flagged or uncertain cases are pushed straight to a licensed doctor's queue in real time — closing the loop from AI to clinician inside one product.

Market Opportunity

Riding a shift already underway.

A large, underserved population

Skin conditions are among the most common reasons people seek medical advice, yet specialist access remains geographically concentrated across India — leaving most of the country reliant on general practitioners or no care at all.

Digital health is the direction of travel

Teleconsultation and digital-first healthcare have moved from novelty to mainstream expectation in India, mirroring the national push toward accessible digital health infrastructure.

India-built, India-first AI

DermaLens AI's models are trained and operated in-house, not resold from a foreign API — positioning it inside the country's push for sovereign, application-layer AI capability.

Land with screening, expand into care

A free, low-friction screening product is a natural top of funnel for every paid layer on the roadmap — consultations, partnerships, and licensing all sit downstream of it.
